The Equipment

How Our Cleaning Truck Works

Six steps from curb to clean. Here's exactly what happens when our truck arrives at your street.

🚛
Step 1 — The Truck Arrives

Our purpose-built mobile cleaning unit arrives at your curb after the City's garbage truck has already passed. The truck is completely self-contained — carrying up to 500 gallons of fresh water and a sealed 300-gallon greywater capture tank. We never need access to your home's water supply.

Self-contained · No hookups needed
🦾
Step 2 — Dual Bin Grabber

This is what separates us from the competition. Our hydraulic dual bin grabber extends and latches onto two bins simultaneously by their handles. Both bins are lifted, inverted, and positioned over the cleaning chamber in one motion — cleaning two bins in the time others spend on one. That efficiency means faster service and lower cost for you.

2 bins at once · Hydraulic lift system
🌡️
Step 3 — High-Pressure Hot Water Spray

With both bins fully inverted, our 360° rotating spray nozzles fire water heated to 160°F+ at high pressure into every corner, crevice, and surface of the bin. The combination of extreme heat and pressure destroys biofilm, removes stuck-on debris, food residue, and kills bacteria that cold water simply cannot touch. No scrubbing required — the physics do the work.

160°F+ · 360° rotating coverage
🧴
Step 4 — Disinfect & Deodorize

After the hot-water blast, we apply EPA-registered disinfectant that kills 99.9% of bacteria — including E. coli, salmonella, and listeria. We then apply our odor-neutralizing treatment that destroys the organic compounds responsible for bad smells at the molecular source. Not a cover-up. Not a fragrance. An actual chemical elimination of the odor.

99.9% bacteria kill · EPA-registered
🌊
Step 5 — Greywater Capture

Every drop of dirty water — including all the bacteria, grime, and cleaning solution — is captured in our sealed 300-gallon greywater tank. Not a drop reaches the curb, the gutter, or the storm drain. In O'ahu, storm drains flow directly to the ocean. We take this seriously. All captured greywater is transported to City & County of Honolulu approved discharge facilities and handled in full compliance with State and County environmental regulations.

Zero discharge · Ocean-safe · Fully compliant
✅
Step 6 — Bins Returned, Job Logged

Both cleaned bins are placed back at your curb. We log the job complete in our system and you receive a notification. Total time at your property: 5–10 minutes. Your bins are sanitized, deodorized, and ready to go back to the garage. We'll be back next month — or next quarter — automatically.

5–10 min service · Automatic recurring
